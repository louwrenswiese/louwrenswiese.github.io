Chapter 3

Major Causes of High Blood Pressure (Ranked by Impact)

Not all causes of high blood pressure matter equally. Some have a small effect, while others drive pressure persistently upward. This chapter ranks the most important contributors based on real-world impact.

Rank 1: Excess Body Fat (Especially Visceral Fat)

Visceral fat — fat stored around the organs — is the strongest predictor of high blood pressure worldwide. Waist circumference often predicts risk better than body weight alone.

Rank 2: Insulin Resistance and High-Glycaemic Diets

Insulin resistance increases sodium retention, inflammation, and arterial stiffness, linking blood pressure closely to blood sugar regulation.

Rank 3: Chronic Stress and Poor Sleep

Persistent stress and inadequate sleep activate pressure-raising hormone systems throughout the day.

Rank 4: High Sodium / Low Potassium Imbalance

Low potassium intake often turns normal salt intake into a problem by impairing sodium excretion.

Rank 5: Physical Inactivity

Regular movement improves arterial flexibility and lowers resting blood pressure, even at moderate intensity.

Rank 6: Smoking and Chronic Inflammation

Smoking and ongoing inflammation accelerate arterial stiffness and vascular damage.

Rank 7: Genetics

Genetics influence baseline risk but rarely determine outcomes on their own.
