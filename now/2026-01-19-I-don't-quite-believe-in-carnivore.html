In response to: Very many YouTube Videos proposing that the (to quote Google), “carnivore diet is an extremely restrictive diet that's grown in popularity mostly via social media as a quick way to lose weight over the last few years. It's made up of only animal products: meat, fish, seafood, eggs and some dairy products like butter.”

I have several objections, tested not only on myself, but also supported by research.

First I want to state my purely emotional objections. There is a country called France where people love to eat. They are known over the world for their cuisine. They also love to drink, and even smoke their Gualoises. They have many other habits that are known to shorten life, such as engaging in desert warfare (“The French Foreign Legion”), sleeping with other people’s spouses, spending whole afternoons having lunch, and being nice to the English.

Yet they live long. They produce writers, musicians, painters, and philosophers. They had a revolution that invented the idea of “democracy”. They happily chopped off the heads of those who disagreed, and a few more. Once one gets worked up, in a crowd, it’s hard to stop. So a few of those philosophers may also have been shortened by a head. But there’s always more.

If one starts to study the French way to prepare your food, the first skill you have to master, is “chopping up vegetables”. The second skill is preparing a lovely soup. Perhaps an onion soup. And so it goes.

When studying French cousine, it is easy to come across an American couple visiting Paris, and meeting French food. They are fairly large people, because back at home there’s a bit of a crisis. Instead of eating small quantities, very well prepared, which is the French way, the poor dear Americans go the other way. Huge quantities prepared as “take-outs”. Uber Eats. You don’t have to get off your couch. Whereas the Frenchman has to jump up every morning and go buy his “baguette” with some fresh ham and cheese, and perhaps some tomato and garden salad.

Now, recently, the “carnivore diet” has ben proposed as a solution to the overdose of carbohydrates that the wealthy countries eat. Eat more meat. Just eat animal products. Vegetables are bad for you. They contain glucose, that will give you heart attacks. And, you can eat as much as you like. So now, we see everyone stuffing themselves on the best grass fed steaks. It’s actually a bit sicking. Especially if you read the comments. “After eating just the best quality free range chicken eggs, wild-caught salmon and grass fed beef burgers for a month, no vegetables and fruit at all, and no bread, my weight went from 380 lbs to 350 lbs. This is amazing. At this rate I will be thin again in 6 months.”

One may argue, that the animals benefit. The chickens roam free, the steers eat high qulity food, and the salmon don’t live in farms. Yes, but then they’re eaten. Perhaps not ther life goal they had in mind.

So that’s my feeling about the carnivore diet. Don’t become a glutton. If yu are one, take up French Cuisine. Not just eating it, but preparing it. Go stand in the kitchen and start chopping. Make all your own food to the highest standards. You will get to know your carrots, cabbage, and salad, as well as your fish and beef steak. But you will treat them with respect. And when you eat them, they will give you pleasure, but not of a gluttony kind. You will eat them with distinction and taste. And your body will show the results.
